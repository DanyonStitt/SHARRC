About

Why do we exist?

New Zealand is facing significant health challenges due to ageing populations, sedentary lifestyles, and unhealthy habits. These factors contribute to a high rate of diseases, including heart disease, diabetes, obesity, cancer, depression, and anxiety, costing over $100 million per condition annually.

Physical activity is essential for improving physical and mental health, but technological innovation is needed to improve equality of access, enhance sports performance, reduce injury risks, and improve rehabilitation outcomes. While the NZ Ministry of Health has several initiatives in place, SHARRC believes that technology can play a crucial role in addressing these challenges.

Who we are

SHARRC is a multidisciplinary team made up of experts in sports physiology and health, data science, engineering, psychology, human interface technology, interactive game design, and law. We use translational approaches to improve sports access, performance, equipment, and rehabilitation outcomes, with the goal of helping people live healthier lives. The team is diverse in experience and seniority, ranging from PhD and postdoctoral research fellows to professors and industry leaders. Their ultimate goal is to achieve Sport NZ's vision of getting everyone active in New Zealand and ensuring access to quality healthcare regardless of background.

Without diverse voices as part of the conversation, it is nearly impossible to discover optimal solutions. The ability to imagine something new or come up with a new solution entirely depends upon involving a variety of viewpoints.

We deliver impact by providing research, enhancing services and developing solutions in the following areas:

Sports Performance

Research and engineering for High Performance training, monitoring, assessment, and coaching as well as supporting injury prevention and rehabilitation. Clients: elite sports clubs, HPSNZ, Para Sport NZ, Crusaders.

Active Lifestyle Solutions

Engineering purpose-built, physical activity solutions for New Zealanders in their everyday lives

Exercise Prescriptions

Working alongside clinicians and in partnership with CDHB to devise individualised exercise prescription programmes to enhance outcomes of patients during and after medical treatment

Rehabilitation Solutions

Rehabilitation research and technology for return to healthy and active lifestyles

Technology

Develop solutions for monitoring physical activity, well-being, and mental health, design devices and sport equipment for the general public, including app & immersive game development, and utilise the existing state-of-the-art capabilities at UC to perform cutting edge research in sport, health, and rehabilitation

Mental Well-being

Researching and actively engaging the social and psychological sciences in regards to the role of sports and physical activity in well-being
