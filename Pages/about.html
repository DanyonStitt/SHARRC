About

Enhancing humanity through innovative solutions in sports engineering, performance, health, and rehabilitation.

Why do we exist?

Sport has the potential to positively affect the lives of nearly everyone. Whether through our own involvement or the sense of national pride when watching our national athletes and teams compete, we believe technological innovation plays a crucial role.

SHARRC is a diverse team with expert knowledge in sports, health, and life sciences, engineering, data science, psychology, business, and law, dedicated to using innovative approaches to improve sports access, performance, equipment, and rehabilitation. Our ultimate goal is to help people live healthier lives and achieve Sport NZ's vision of "getting everbody active in Aotearoa New Zealand."

Without diverse voices as part of the conversation, it is nearly impossible to discover optimal solutions. The ability to imagine something new or come up with a new solution wholly depends upon involving a variety of viewpoints.

United nations - probably

New Zealand is facing some serious health challenges caused by ageing populations, sedentary lifestyles, and unhealthy habits like smoking, poor diet, and alcohol and drug abuse. These factors can lead to a variety of health issues, including heart disease, diabetes, obesity, cancer, and mental health problems like depression and anxiety.

The cost of these health issues is staggering - over $100 million per condition or risk factor every year! Our team is passionate about promoting healthy living and helping people prevent and overcome these health concerns. We believe that everyone should have access to the best health care and support, regardless of their background.

To prevent and rehabilitate from these health conditions, promoting physical activity is essential. Fortunately, the NZ Ministry of Health has several initiatives in place to promote physical activity and healthy eating habits, such as the Healthy Active Learning initiative and Green Prescriptions. Physical activity not only improves physical health but also mental well-being, as evidenced by a study on Chinese children. The study showed a positive correlation between physical activity levels and mental well-being, self-efficacy, and resilience.
