About

Enhancing humanity through innovative solutions in sports engineering, performance, health, and rehabilitation.

Why do we exist?

Sport has the potential to positively affect the lives of nearly everyone. Whether through our own involvement or the sense of national pride when watching our national athletes and teams compete, we believe technological innovation plays a crucial role.

SHARRC is a diverse team with expert knowledge in sports, health, and life sciences, engineering, data science, psychology, business, and law, dedicated to using innovative approaches to improve sports access, performance, equipment, and rehabilitation. Our ultimate goal is to help people live healthier lives and achieve Sport NZ's vision of "getting everbody active in Aotearoa New Zealand." Our team is passionate about promoting healthy living and helping people prevent and overcome these health concerns. We believe that everyone should have access to the best health care and support, regardless of their background.

Without diverse voices as part of the conversation, it is nearly impossible to discover optimal solutions. The ability to imagine something new or come up with a new solution wholly depends upon involving a variety of viewpoints.

Challenges

New Zealand is facing some serious health challenges caused by ageing populations, sedentary lifestyles, and unhealthy habits like smoking, poor diet, or alcohol abuse. These factors contribute to a high rates of heart disease, diabetes, obesity, cancer, and mental health problems like depression and anxiety, costing over $100 million per condition or risk factor every year!

Physical activity is essential to not only improve physical health but also mental well-being, self-efficacy, and resilience. While the NZ Ministry of Health has several initiatives in place to promote physical activity and healthy eating habits, such as the Healthy Active Learning initiative and Green Prescriptions, Technological innovation is a powerful tool to improve equality of access, enhance sports performance, redesign equipment and apparel, reduce risk of injury, and improve outcomes for rehabilitation.

Who are we?

The proposed team has a significant diversity of experience, from sports physiology, physiological modelling, well-being, human factors and behavioural science, through to human interface technology and interactive game design. To achieve the ambitious goals within this proposal, this diverse team is essential to success. The project team also includes a diverse range of seniority, from postdoctoral research fellows and lecturers, through to Professors
