About

Enhancing humanity through innovative solutions in sports engineering, sports performance, health, and rehabilitation to active lifestyles.
Sport has the potential to positively affect the lives of nearly every New Zealander, whether it be through the sense of national pride that arises through watching our national athletes and teams compete or through our own involvement in health via sports and physical activity. SHARRC is a multi- and inter-disciplinary research cluster, which combines domains of sports, health sciences, engineering, design, data science, life sciences, human-computer interaction, social and psychological sciences, business, and law. Without diverse voices as part of the conversation, it is nearly impossible to discover optimal solutions. According to CERIC in Canada, diversity leads to innovation [17]. The ability to imagine something new or come up with a new solution wholly depends upon a variety of viewpoints involved in the brainstorming, design, testing, manufacture, and feedback.
We believe technological innovation plays a crucial role in improving sports access, performance, equipment, and rehabilitation outcomes. We are committed to using innovative approaches to improve health outcomes and assist with Sport NZ's vision of "getting Every Body Active in Aotearoa New Zealand."
New Zealand is facing some serious health challenges caused by ageing populations, sedentary lifestyles, and unhealthy habits like smoking, poor diet, and alcohol and drug abuse. These factors can lead to a variety of health issues, including heart disease, diabetes, obesity, cancer, and mental health problems like depression and anxiety.
The cost of these health issues is staggering - over $100 million per condition or risk factor every year! Our team is passionate about promoting healthy living and helping people prevent and overcome these health concerns. We believe that everyone should have access to the best health care and support, regardless of their background.
To prevent and rehabilitate from these health conditions, promoting physical activity is essential. Fortunately, the NZ Ministry of Health has several initiatives in place to promote physical activity and healthy eating habits, such as the Healthy Active Learning initiative and Green Prescriptions. Physical activity not only improves physical health but also mental well-being, as evidenced by a study on Chinese children. The study showed a positive correlation between physical activity levels and mental well-being, self-efficacy, and resilience.
